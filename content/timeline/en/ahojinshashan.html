Some instances of glorious history of Jainims

2577 years ago today, Shraman Bhagwan Mahavir established four-fold Sangha on the day of Vaishakh Shukla 11 in Pavapuri. Although there were grave dangers to Jain Dharma for two thousand years from the Nirvana of Lord Veer due to the ill effects of the planet Bhasma, but history testifies that Jain Dharam has a glorious history. Thousands of episodes in Jain scriptures are written in golden letters. Some instances are presented to inspire us. :-

-30

Shasan Sthapana

At the age of 42, Shraman Bhagwan Mahavir established Chaturvidh Sangh on the day of Vaishakh Shukla 11 in Pavapuri. Indrabhuti and other distinguished 11 Brahmins were assigned the rank of Gandhar. Chandanbala became the first Sadhviji, Anand etc. shravak and revati etc.. shravika.

First Head of Monks

Nine Gandhars attained salvation before Lord Mahavir Swami. Lord's Nirvana was followed by Gautamswamiji's Nirvana. For this reason, his disciple tradition came under the guidance of Fifth Gandhar Sudharma Swamiji. For this reason the current Shramana tradition belongs to Sudharmaswamiji

Shrimal and Porwal community

The fifth Pattadhar of Sri Parshwanath Bhagavan, Sri Swayamprabhasuri went to a yagna (a ritual done in front of a sared fire) which was held in Srimal Nagri and Padmavati Nagri and preached to the king and his subjects and turned 135,000 house-bearers Jain and henceforth established the Shrimal and Porwal dynasty.

64

Last Kevali

Jambuswami was the last kevali of this Avsarpini period in Bharat region. Hearing the sermon of Sri Sudharmaswamy at the age of 16, he accepted celibacy. After 20 years of diksha, he attained kevalgyan and spending 44 years as Kevali he attained salvation at the age of 80 years.

70

Oswal Community

The 6th Pattadhar of Shri Parshwanath Bhagavan, Sri Ratnaprabhasuri established the Upkesh community by teaching 1,80,000 Kshatriyas in Upkesh Nagar. Upkesh Nagar later began to be called as Osian and this community Oswal. Also, Oswal, Porwal and Srimal were popularly known as Mahajan.

160

First council of Agam

After 12 years of drought, the Shramana Sangh under the guidance and leadership of Shri Sthulibhadra held the first agam council in Veer Nirvana Samvat 160 at Pataliputra. He collected 11 aagams and to learn the 12th Anga Sri Sthulibhadra and other munis went to Sri Bhadrabahu Swami.

222

Last Shrutkevali

Shri Prabhav, Sri Shayambhav, Sri Yashobhadra, Shri Sambhut, Shri Bhadrabahu and Shri Sthulabhadra, these six are known as Shrutakevali because they knew all 14 purvas. Shri Bhadrabahu was the last shrut kevali who knew both Sutra and meaning and Shri Sthulabhadra knew only Sutra of last 4 purvas.

292

Construction of 1.25 lakh Jain temples

Learning how he attained the kingdom by the grace of Arya Suhasti, his benefactor from previous birth, Samprati Maharaja built 1.25 lakh jain temples, 1.25 crore statues, 95,000 metal statues, renovated 36000 jain temples and graced the land of India with 700 charitable institutions.

374

Elephant cave in Khandgiri, Orissa

An inscription about Kharavela a devout Jain still exists in Khandagiri, Orissa. It is written that Kharavela defeated Pushyamitra and took the statue of Rishabhdev famously known as Kalinga Jin. This statue was built by Shrenik Maharaja and made auspicious by Sudharma Swami in Kumaragiri.

375

2nd Agam Vachna

On Kumaragiri, Kalingaraj Kharavel convened the 2nd Agam Vachna Conference under the guidance of Arya Susthitsuri and Supratibaddhsuuri. In this Arya Belissah composed ‘Angavidya’, Sri Swati composed ‘Tattvaratha’ and Mr. Shyamacharya composed ‘Pragyapana’ sutra.

470

Vikram Samvat

Shri Siddheshna Diwakar Suri brought forth the idol of Shri Avanti Parshwanath by composing the Sri Kalyan Mandir Stotra. In this way by enlightening him Vikram Raja became a devout Jain. He established his own calendar which is called Vikram Samvatsar.

580*

3rd Agam Vachna

During the 12-year drought period, most Sadhus including Vajraswami left for heaven. The 10th Purv was also lost. Under the leadership of Vajrasensuuri, Nandilasuri and Rakshitsuri ji, 3rd agam vachna was held and after a few years, the Shramana Sangha was arranged in four clans.

  1. Read More
830

4th Agam Vachna

At that time there was a period of turbulence - hegemony of Buddhists in Saurashtra and war between Huns and Gupts in North. Later, Shiladityas won in Vallabhi and Guptas conquered Mathura. Shramana congregated under the guidance of Nagarjunasuri in Mathura and Skandil Suri in Vallabhi.

980

5th Agam Vachnan

The 5th Agam Vachna was held under the direction of Shri Devardhigini Shamashraman and Acharya Bhootdinna. For the first time, the Aagams were written down. In the gathering, 84 verses, niyrukti, bhashya, kampyapadi and elemental sutras and crores of shlokes were written.

983*

Kalpasutra Open Preaching

Senapati Bhattarak became the king of Vallabhi by taking advantage of the weakness of the Gupta dynasty. His son Dhruvasen came t the throne of Vallabhi. He followed Jainism. Kalpasutra's first open sermon were given in Kumarabhukti Nagar, Anandpur to console a mourning Dhruvsen over death of his son.

1272

Establishment of Patan

Vanaraj established Anhilpur Patan and constructed the temple of Panchasara Parshwanath Bhagwan. Acharya Shilgunsuri played an important role in the rise of Vanaraj. Vanraj was fully supported by the Jains - brave Chamraj, Nina and Ashak were Jain ministers of Vanaraja

1558

Vimalvasahi

Minister Vimal was the commander of King Bhimdev of Gujarat. Once influenced by Sri Dharmaghoshasuri, he wanted to atone for the sins of wars. Acharya Shri asked him to renovate Abu Tirth. So he built a magnificient temple with amazing artwork, due to which Abu is renowned in the world.

1612

Antariksh Parshwanath

A Parshwanath idol built in clay during Ramayana, was worshiped by a Dev in Vidarbha. Elchipur King, suffering from leprosy, was cured by it. The king tried to take it to his city. However, it stayed put in the way. So he built a temple right there, now known as Antriksh Parshwanath.

1669

Kumarpal

Kumarapal conquered 14 countries post his coronation on the throne of Gujarat. With the preaching of Acharya Shri Hemachandrasuri, Kumarapal proclaimed ahimsa, took the vows of Jain Shravak, made seven pilgrimage, constructed 21 libraries and 1444 new temples and renovated 1800 temples.

1683

Shatrunjay's 14th Renovation

Bahad was the minister of Siddharaj and an officer of high rank of Kumarapal. He rebuilt the wooden temple using marble. He got it consecrated by Hemachandrasuri. This temple still exists today. The news bearer of the temple’s construction progress was given 16 silver tongues.

1746

Vastupal and Tejpla

In the beginning the brothers were ministers of King Veeradhaval. Their shrewdness led to the re-organization of the Hindu kings of Gujarat, Rajasthan and Maharashtra. Together they won a total of 63 wars. They considered Acahrya Sri Vijayanasuri as their religious mentor.

1755

Tapagaccha

Sri Jagachandrasuri was the 44th in the lineage of Shri Sudharma Swami. While doing ayambil for twelve years, he came to Mewar. Rana Jaitrasinh was impressed with him and honoured him with the title "Tapa". Hence, the Shramana tradition of Lord Mahavira started being called as Tapagachh.

1780

Renovation of Shankeshwar Tirth

The king of Jhinjhuvada suffered from leprosy and to cure that he tried several remedies. He finally prayed to Suryanarayana said – Worship the God in Shankheshwar and you will be cured of the disease. The King renovated the shrine in Vik. Sam. in 1310.

1966

Ranakpur

Once Sanghvi Dharanshah saw the Nalinigulam-Viman in a dream and decided to build as a magnificent temple as he saw. Later, a grand three-storey 45-feet tall temple was built by him in Ranakpur with 14 pillars which was inaugurated by Acharya Somasundarasuri in Vik. Sam. 1496.

2047

16th Renovation of Shatrunjay

Muslim aggressors broke the main idol on Shatrunjaya. Karma Shah of Udaipur built a new idol at an auspicious time, which was consecrated by Shri Vidyamandanasuri. At the time of pratistha, God breathed seven times. This idol is the mulnayak on the peak of Shatrunjaya today.

2078

Akbar

Preached by Acharya Shri Hiravijayuri, Akbar proclaimed 6 months of non violence every year in his 12 districts, stopped "jaziya", stopped confiscating wealth of childless couple, stopped consuming the tongue of 500 birds in his breakfast and honored Acharya Sri with the title of Jagadguru.

2378

Protection of Samvegi Path

Shri Budhvijay opposed accumulation of wealth by sadhus and residence in Jain Temple and revived Murti Puja. Facing hardshop alone, he revived the true path of Tirthankar Kevali Bhagwant

2547

Today's Sadhu Samuday

Today, there are 17,155 Sadhu – Sadhviji combined in all the four major sects, including Svetambara, Digambar Sthanakwasi and Terapanti. The Shwetambar Murtipujak is the largest of the Sadhu community with a total of around 10715 monks.
