Aho Jinshashan!

2577 years ago today, Shraman Bhagwan Mahavir established four-fold Sangha on the day of Vaishakh Shukla 11 in Pavapuri. Although there were grave dangers to Jain Dharma for two thousand years from the Nirvana of Lord Veer due to the ill effects of the planet Bhasma, but history testifies that Jain Dharam has a glorious history. Thousands of episodes in Jain scriptures are written in golden letters. Some instances are presented to inspire us.

Khema Derani

In the times of Muhmud Begada there was a severe drought. During the time span of the drought that lasted one year Khema Seth of Hadala donated food to all the needy in the entire state and retained the title of “Shah” for the Mahajans.

Javad Shah

This jain philanthropist lived in the first century of Vikram savant. It has been centuries, but yet the fame of this brave man is still long-lasting in jain history. The great Shri Dhaneshwarsuri describes his achievements in great detail in his popular granth "Shatrunjay Mahatmay". In addition other authors too have portrayed glory of this glorious man. Receiving this level of respect from jain saints, who have attained immense knowledge and foresight, itself signifies the greatness and dignity of Javad.

Mahajan Sangh

The Mahajan Sangh have always held high positions in the society. This was because, whether in India or outside, whether in running the Kingdom, or in winning the war, or in trade, or in philanthropy or at times of famine or external aggression or misrule or oppressive emperors — they were brave and popular in the society due to their generosity. This was the reason the Emperors, the great Kings, Maharaja, Badshah bestowed important titles like Jagat Seth, Nagar Seth, Panch, Chowtia, Chaudhary and Tikayat etc to the Mahajans.
